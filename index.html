02 Target Defect Classes
01
Scratches
Linear surface abrasions caused by contact with abrasive materials during handling or machining. Appear as elongated bright streaks in grayscale imagery.
02
Rolled-in Scale
Oxide scale pressed into the metal surface during hot rolling, visible as irregular dark patches with distinct texture contrast against base metal.
03
Pitting
Small localized depressions or cavities on the surface caused by corrosion or mechanical impacts. Detected via circular shape descriptors and intensity dips.
04
Inclusion
Non-metallic foreign particles embedded in the steel matrix, appearing as irregular dark regions with high local texture contrast.
05
Patches
Irregularly shaped surface regions with different reflectivity from the surrounding base metal, often caused by uneven cooling or coating defects.
06
Crazing
Network of fine surface cracks forming a craze pattern. Distinguished from scratches by their branching, non-linear morphology and dense clustering.

04 Why Classical ML ‚Äî Not Deep Learning
Small Dataset Suitability
MSMEs typically have 100‚Äì500 labeled samples per defect class. Classical models generalize well without requiring thousands of annotated images.
Interpretability
Quality assurance teams need to understand why a part was rejected. Feature importance rankings and LDA projections provide transparent explanations.
Hardware Independence
Entire inference pipeline runs on standard CPU hardware. No GPU, no CUDA, no cloud dependency ‚Äî deployable on any inspection station.
Fast Inference
Classical feature extraction + nearest centroid classification achieves sub-100ms inference per image ‚Äî suitable for production line integration.
